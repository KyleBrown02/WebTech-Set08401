Chicken Teriyaki

    Ingredients

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs, sliced into strips
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • Steamed rice and steamed broccoli, to serve

    • Cooking method

    1. In a medium bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, mirin, sake, and brown sugar until the sugar has dissolved.
    2. Add the chicken strips to the bowl and toss to coat in the marinade. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours.
    3. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for 1 minute, or until fragrant.
    4 Using a slotted spoon, remove the chicken from the marinade and add it to the skillet. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, or until browned on all sides and cooked through.
    5. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and water to make a slurry. Pour the slurry into the skillet and stir until the sauce thickens, about 1-2 minutes.
    6. Serve the chicken teriyaki over steamed rice and with steamed broccoli on the side.
