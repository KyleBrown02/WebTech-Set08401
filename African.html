Jollof Rice

    Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 3 medium-sized fresh tomatoes, diced
  • 2 red bell peppers, diced
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (optional)
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro leaves, chopped (optional)

    • Cooking method

    1. Rinse the rice under cold water and drain. Set aside.
    2. In a blender, puree the diced tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers (if using), and onion until smooth.
    3. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the garlic and saut√© for 30 seconds, stirring constantly.
    4. Add the pureed vegetables to the pot and stir to combine. Cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened and darkened in color.
    5. Add the tomato paste, smoked paprika, cumin, salt, and black pepper to the pot. Stir to combine.
    6. Add the rice to the pot and stir to coat it in the sauce.
    7. Add the broth to the pot and bring to a boil.
    8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid has been absorbed.
    9. Remove the pot from heat and let it sit, covered, for 5-10 minutes.
    10. Fluff the rice with a fork and sprinkle with chopped cilantro (if using) before serving.
    Enjoy your delicious Jollof Rice, a flavorful and spicy dish that is a favorite across many African countries!
